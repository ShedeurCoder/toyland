The 2022 Toyland Presidential Election

The 2022 Toyland Presidential Election is the consistent election for the next president of Toyland. There are three people currently running in this election: Current president Robert Duck, former Legoland governor Jorgen Wurtz, and Toyland Communist Party leader Charles de Marcs.

All candidates

President

Robert Duck

President Robert Duck is running for re-election and stands in the center-right of the political spectrum. His biggest accomplishment is establishing diplomacy with over 100 countries.

Jorgen Wurtz

Former governor Jorgen Wurtz is running for president for the first time and stands in the center-left of the political spectrum. His biggest accomplishment is re-unifying Jorgenberg and Toyland.

Charles de Marcs

Toyland Communist Party (TCP) leader Charles de Marcs is running for president for the second time and stands in the far-left of the political spectrum. His biggest accomplishment is leading the TCP through a Communist revolution in a small city in Moano.

Vice President

Karl Herrmann

The running mate of Duck, Karl Herrmann, is the current vice president of the country, running for re-election. His biggest accomplishment is raising 300 billion USD for schools in Nigeria, Sudan, and other poor African Countries.

James Williams

The running mate of Wurtz, James Williams, is the former mayor of Legoland City. His biggest accomplishment was declining the poverty percentage by 50%.

Louis Lénine

The running mate of de Marcs, Louis Lénine, is the former leader of the Toyland Communist Party (TCP). His biggest accomplishment is establishing a Communist government in a small town on KJ Island.

Presidential Debate

The presidential debate will air on Saturday, May 21, 2022 at 12 pm TCT.

Election day

The election is on Wednesday, June 1st, 2022 from 6 am to 9 pm nationwide for all time zones. The day will be a no work and no school day.

Election booths will be in your city's central building. In the case of big cities, there will be 2 or more booths near each other in the central building.
